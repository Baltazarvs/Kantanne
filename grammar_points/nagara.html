Parallel Actions 「ながら」

Syntax

連用形: う→い
+
ながら
、
...

*連用形 is verb base that is made by switching verb ending to い hiragana column.

ながら is a particle that is used to specify parallel action in sentence. It links two actions where they take place simultaneously. For example - 'She sung crying.' (She singed and cried at same time).
As said, 「ながら」 is used for this structure and follows some rules before being attached.

例
お母かあさんが部屋へやを掃除そうじしながら、音楽おんがくを聞きいた。
Mother cleaned house while listening to music.
運転うんてんしながら、電話でんわを使つかわない方ほうがいい。
You should not use phone while driving.

Difference between 「ながら」and 「間 / 間に」

Some people would find this rather confusing than clear as both these grammar points cover the sense of "parallel actions". Thankfully, there are differences between these two.

  • ながら is used for two actions that are taking place at the same time. But, both of them are done by the same subject.
  • 間/間に is used for two parallel actions too, but... it is not necessarily done by the same subject. And there may be differences between action durations.
