Sentence Word Order

Word order of Japanese sentence is fixed in three basic parts and they are subject object verb . Verb is always located at the end of sentence and there are no exceptions regarding its position.

Basic Order

As said, Japanese sentences follow subject object verb word order. It is different from Western languages that mainly follow SVO structure. A simple example of such sentence would look like following example.

例
猫が 魚を 食べる。
Cat eats fish.

Subject is marked with particle が.

Object is marked with particle を.

Verb is the final part and ends with kana from う row.

In mentioned example: Cat (subject) eats (predicate) fish (object). A subject is executing action of eating and fish is target of the action.

Topic (は)

Topic is one of primary parts in Japanese sentences. They are marked with particle 「は」. Topic we are taking about is nearly always known and is recognizable part of sentence, because topic must be known in order to talk about it. It means that question word such as 何, 誰, どれ, どんあ, etc. are most often used with particle 「が」. If used with GA, then, answer must be also marked with GA.

- Certain topic is introduced only once in conversation. In order to change topic, one must be introduced using 「は」 again.

- It is roughly translated as "speaking of...", "as for...".

- If 「は」 is used with question words, it adds contrast to sentence. It implies that there is group of people and makes implication. So using GA with question words simply asks question and looks for subject. 「は」 is used for contrast and emphasizing from group.

例
A: 昨日家に誰だれは入はいったの？
B: 僕は入ったんだよ。
A: (Others maybe did not but as for one) Who entered the house yesterday?
B: I did (implying that others maybe did not, but you are confirming for yourself).
A: 昨日家に誰が入ったの？
B: 僕が入ったんだよ。
A: Who entered the house yesterday?
B: I did (I entered the house).

This is good example on what is difference between 「は」 and 「が」.

NOTE: Always try to translate literally from Japanese and try to think in Japanese instead of relocating and reversing sentence order. It will help you not mess up your brain.

Time (に)

Time specification in sentence is simple and naturally appears at two parts of sentence: either as first part of sentence or after topic. It has mutliple specifications where most common ones are:

  • Time Nouns (一昨日、昨日、今日、明日、明後日、来年...)
  • Clock Time (4:30 PM, 15:45)
  • Date Time (2025年6月8日)
  • 後 (ご) / 後で (あとで)、前 (まえ)
  • ～時、の間 / の間に


例
夏なつの間あいだに、アイスクリームを食たべたい。
I want to eat ice cream during summer.
彼が午前７時半ごぜんななじはんに私のお弁当べんとうを食べてしまいました。
He ate my lunch in 7:30 AM.
一昨日おとといから今日きょうまで、同おなじ食べ物たべものを食べているなあ。
From day before yesterday until today I've been eating the same food, you know!
二ヶ月にかげつ経たったのに、まだ利益りえきがないよ。もう終おわりにするなあ。
Two months have passed and, still, no profit! I want to quit already!
