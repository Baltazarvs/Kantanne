Conditionals are one of most important parts of Japanese grammar. They are sentences where certain action will be executed once condition is satisfied. Sometimes, even Japanese struggle to determine which conditional should be used, so usage can often be subjective choice.

Contents:
  • たら Conditional
  • ば Conditional
  • と Conditional
  • なら Conditional

と Definite Conditional

This is perhaps the easiest conditional to understand and use since it has simple usage. It is used for actions that occur independently of our control. Their actions are automatic consequences when condition is satisfied. For example: natural occurrences, actions we cannot control, など...

Syntax

詞 Word Type + と
Verb (辞書形) - と
い Adjective - と
な Adjective だ と
名詞 Noun だ と


例
夏だと暑くなる。
When it is summer, it gets hot.
巨大な岩が落ちるとうるさい音がする。
When big rock falls, a loud sound occurs (it makes a loud sound).

たら Real/Hypothetical Conditional

たら conditional is perhaps the most commonly used conditional in Japanese. It is interpreted in different ways according to contexts. It can be "when", "if" or "after". It exclusively requires past form of the verb.

例
そのビスケットを食べたら、毒どくに当あたるよ。
If you ate that biscuit, you would get poisoned.

As we can see, 神 has first syllable accented or pitch-voiced. 紙 and 髪 have exactly the same.
